§ I  ·  Exchange Framework

IRC §1031 — Statutory Authority and Mechanics

IRC §1031 permits deferral of capital gains recognition on the sale of investment real property, provided proceeds are reinvested into qualifying like-kind replacement property. The gain is deferred, not forgiven — it carries forward until a taxable disposition occurs outside a qualifying exchange structure.

  • —Applies to real property held for investment or productive use in a trade or business — not primary residences or dealer inventory
  • —Defers federal capital gains, IRC §1250 depreciation recapture, Net Investment Income Tax (3.8%), and applicable state-level capital gains taxes
  • —Full deferral requires reinvestment of all net equity and replacement of relinquished debt at equivalent or greater levels — any shortfall constitutes taxable boot
  • —No statutory limit on successive exchanges — deferred basis carries forward indefinitely through qualifying reinvestments
  • —Step-up in cost basis at death may reduce or eliminate accumulated deferred gain under current federal estate law, subject to legislative change

§1031 is a deferral mechanism. The decision of what to exchange into is structurally independent from the decision to exchange.

§ II  ·  Statutory Timelines

Identification and Acquisition Deadlines

Two hard deadlines govern every §1031 exchange. Both are measured from the relinquished property closing date and neither is extendable by election.

45
Days
Identification Period. Written identification of replacement properties must be delivered to the Qualified Intermediary within 45 calendar days. Subject to the Three-Property Rule, 200% Rule, and 95% Exception under Treasury regulations.
180
Days
Exchange Period. Replacement property acquisition must close within 180 calendar days of the relinquished property closing, or by the federal tax return due date for the year of disposition — whichever is earlier.

A Qualified Intermediary must be engaged before the relinquished property closes. Constructive receipt of funds by the taxpayer at any point disqualifies the exchange.

§ III  ·  DST Legal Structure

Delaware Statutory Trust — Legal Structure and IRS Authority

A Delaware Statutory Trust holds direct fee title to real property. In Revenue Ruling 2004-86, the IRS confirmed that a beneficial interest in a qualifying DST constitutes like-kind replacement property for purposes of IRC §1031.

  • —The trust holds fee title — investors hold undivided beneficial interests, which constitute the §1031 replacement property
  • —Beneficial owners retain no management rights, voting authority, or operational control — the trustee or master tenant governs the asset
  • —Debt is held at the trust level — investors are not party to the loan and carry no personal liability for trust obligations in standard structure
  • —DST beneficial interests are securities — offered only through properly licensed channels to accredited investors under applicable federal and state securities law
  • —Rev. Rul. 2004-86 enumerates permissible trustee actions (the "Seven Deadly Sins") — compliance with these restrictions is required for §1031 qualification to hold

The passive structure of DST ownership is what makes it qualify as like-kind replacement property — and what eliminates management obligations for the investor.

§ IV  ·  Medical Office Real Estate

MOB Asset Class Characteristics

Medical office buildings occupy a structurally distinct position within commercial real estate — driven by healthcare delivery demand rather than consumer spending or corporate space absorption.

  • —Demand anchored by population demographics and healthcare utilization — not subject to the same discretionary economic drivers as retail or traditional office
  • —Extended lease terms attributable to capital intensity of clinical buildout and operational friction of healthcare tenant relocation
  • —Healthcare-system-affiliated tenancy carries distinct credit and lease structure characteristics relative to independent medical occupants
  • —On-campus vs. off-campus designation affects tenant retention dynamics, health system integration, and repositioning considerations

Asset class characteristics described above are general in nature and do not constitute a projection of performance, occupancy, or return for any specific property or offering.

Program Structure

Organizational and Structural Characteristics

The following describes how this program is organized — distribution architecture, sponsor participation, asset focus, and management structure. These are structural characteristics, not performance representations.

Distribution Architecture

Direct Sponsorship — No Broker-Dealer Distribution Layer

This program operates as a direct sponsorship structure without a registered broker-dealer distribution network, wholesaling organization, or registered representative compensation layer. Offerings are made directly to accredited investors with whom a substantive pre-existing relationship has been established under Regulation D.

Absence of broker-dealer compensation layers affects fee composition. All fees and recipients are disclosed in applicable offering documents.

Sponsor Participation

Principal Co-Investment in the Same Trust Structure

Sponsor principals acquire beneficial interests in the same trust as investors, on equivalent economic terms — subject to the same hold period, exit timing, and distribution structure. This produces shared economic exposure at the asset level from formation through disposition.

Co-investment is not present in all programs. Terms are disclosed in offering documents and should be confirmed prior to any investment decision.

Asset Focus

Medical Office Real Estate — Institutional-Credit Tenancy

The program focuses exclusively on medical office real estate. Target acquisitions carry healthcare-system-affiliated or institutional-credit tenancy — regional health systems, academic medical centers, and large independent medical groups. The program does not pursue diversified commercial real estate across unrelated asset types.

Asset class focus reflects the sponsor's sourcing relationships and operational history. It is not a guarantee of tenancy quality or performance in any specific offering.

Management Structure

Third-Party Independent Property Management

Property and asset management is conducted exclusively by independent third-party firms. The sponsoring entity holds no affiliated management company and collects no ongoing management fee income from trust assets. Operational decisions at the property level are made by managers with no financial relationship to the sponsoring entity beyond their management contract.

Management arrangements vary by offering. Identity, independence, and fee structure of all management entities are disclosed in applicable offering documents.

Due Diligence Reference

DST Sponsor Evaluation Framework

Structural questions, evaluation dimensions, and a distribution model comparison — for investors and advisors conducting program-level diligence before any capital commitment.

Fee Architecture
What is the total load on this offering — front-end, ongoing, and disposition — as a percentage of invested capital?+
Total cost reviewed across all fee types over the full hold period provides a more complete picture than any single line item. Acquisition fees, syndication costs, asset management fees, and disposition fees should each be identified and totaled.
Are asset management fees paid to the sponsor or an independent third party? Are the entities affiliated?+
Where management fees flow to a sponsor-affiliated entity, a structural relationship exists between management fee income and operational decisions throughout the hold period. Offering documents should identify the management entity and disclose any affiliation.
Does the sponsor co-invest principal capital in the same trust structure as investors?+
Sponsor co-investment on equivalent economic terms produces shared economic exposure at the asset level. Its presence, extent, and specific terms should be confirmed in offering documents — it is not present in all programs.
Was an independent third-party appraisal obtained, and what was its date relative to trust formation?+
The relationship between the trust's acquisition price and independently appraised value is a standard diligence item. Appraisals and their dates should be disclosed in offering documents.
Management and Conflicts
Who controls the decision to dispose of the asset, and what criteria govern that determination?+
Under DST structure, beneficial owners hold passive interests and do not participate in disposition decisions. The trust agreement should specify the trustee's authority, conditions triggering a sale, and any hold period extension provisions.
Does the sponsor or any affiliated entity have a financial interest in extending the hold period beyond the stated term?+
Fee structures that continue or increase during extended hold periods create a financial relationship between hold duration and sponsor revenue. Review offering documents for provisions affecting the sponsor's economic neutrality on disposition timing.
Asset and Tenancy
What is the credit profile of the anchor tenant, and at what entity level is the lease guaranteed?+
Whether the lease is guaranteed by a health system parent, an operating subsidiary, or a local practice entity affects the credit profile of the income stream. Offering documents should identify the guaranteeing entity and its relationship to the operating tenant.
What is the remaining primary lease term at formation, and how does it compare to the stated hold period?+
A mismatch between remaining lease term and stated hold period introduces rollover risk within the investment horizon. Renewal option terms, conditions, and historical renewal rates for the tenant type are relevant to this analysis.
Sponsor Track Record
How many full-cycle transactions has the sponsor completed — from acquisition through investor distribution?+
Full-cycle completions provide a basis for evaluating operational and exit execution. Projected performance at offering and realized outcomes at exit are distinct data points — both should be reviewed where available.
Is the sponsor a direct issuer, a co-sponsor, or a downstream distributor within a larger syndication structure?+
The sponsor's role within the offering structure affects fee composition and the degree of operational control retained by the presenting firm. The PPM should identify all parties, roles, and compensation.

Common Questions

What property owners ask before engaging.

How does distribution structure affect fee composition?+
DST programs distributed through broker-dealer networks include compensation at multiple distribution levels — dealer manager, wholesaler, and registered representative. These fees are embedded in the offering and reduce investor capital deployed into the underlying asset. Programs operating without a BD distribution layer may carry a different fee composition. Total cost across all fee types is disclosed in applicable offering documents and should be reviewed prior to any commitment.
What is the typical hold period, and who controls the disposition decision?+
Most DSTs are structured with an intended hold of five to seven years — this reflects operational intent, not a guarantee of timing. Beneficial owners hold passive interests and do not participate in disposition decisions. The trust agreement specifies the trustee's authority to initiate a sale, the conditions governing that determination, and any provisions permitting hold period extension. Investors should review those provisions carefully in offering documents.
What does principal co-investment mean structurally?+
Principal co-investment refers to the sponsoring entity acquiring beneficial interests in the same trust on the same economic terms as investors — subject to the same hold, the same exit, and the same distribution structure. This produces shared economic exposure at the asset level. Co-investment is not a uniform characteristic of DST programs generally; its presence, extent, and terms are disclosed in offering documents.
Are DST investments restricted to accredited investors?+
Yes. DST beneficial interests are securities under federal law and are offered only to accredited investors as defined by the SEC under Regulation D. Accredited status is established through income thresholds, net worth thresholds, or qualifying professional certifications. This is a regulatory requirement applicable to all DST offerings.
Can DST interests be held through a self-directed retirement account?+
DST interests may in certain circumstances be held through self-directed IRAs or other qualified retirement accounts. However, the §1031 exchange tax deferral benefit does not apply to assets held within retirement accounts — those accounts are governed by separate tax treatment under the IRC. UBTI implications, custodian requirements, and applicable tax treatment should be reviewed with a qualified tax advisor before proceeding.
How quickly can a DST close relative to exchange deadlines?+
Pre-formed DSTs — in which the trust is already organized and the asset acquired — may close within a significantly shorter timeframe than a direct real property acquisition. This can be relevant for investors approaching the 45-day identification deadline or operating within the 180-day exchange window. Actual closing timelines depend on the specific program and subscription process and should be confirmed before relying on a DST to meet exchange deadlines.
Does a DST investor carry personal liability for the trust's debt?+
Under standard DST structure, the mortgage is held at the trust level. Beneficial owners are not party to the loan, do not execute personal guarantees, and carry no personal liability for trust debt obligations in the typical structure. Specific debt and liability terms should be confirmed in the applicable trust agreement and offering documents.
Are DST interests liquid?+
No. DST beneficial interests are illiquid securities with no established secondary market. Transfer is subject to significant restrictions under applicable securities law and the trust agreement. Investors should treat a DST commitment as capital locked for the duration of the stated hold period. Illiquidity is a material characteristic that should be evaluated in the context of an investor's overall financial circumstances and liquidity requirements.
At DST disposition, can investors independently elect a new §1031 exchange?+
Yes. Upon disposition, each beneficial owner receives their pro-rata share of net proceeds and may independently elect a new §1031 exchange into any qualifying replacement property — another DST, a direct acquisition, or another qualifying structure. There is no obligation to reinvest with the original sponsor. The exchange must be properly structured through a QI and must satisfy all applicable identification and acquisition deadlines.
How does §1031 exchange deferral interact with estate planning?+
Successive §1031 exchanges permit indefinite deferral of recognized gain through continuous reinvestment. Under current federal tax law, assets held at death may receive a step-up in cost basis, potentially eliminating accumulated deferred gain. This outcome depends on estate law as it exists at the time of the decedent's passing and remains subject to legislative change. Investors should review the interaction of §1031 deferral with their specific estate planning objectives with qualified estate and tax counsel.
